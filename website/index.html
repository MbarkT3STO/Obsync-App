Features

Everything your vault needs

Built for Obsidian power users who want their notes safe, versioned, and accessible everywhere.

Auto-Sync

Obsync watches your vault for changes and automatically pushes them after you stop typing. Configurable debounce and poll intervals keep it smart, not noisy.

Background watcher · Chokidar

Secure by Design

Tokens are AES-encrypted locally using Electron's safeStorage. They never touch our servers — or any server except your chosen provider.

AES · safeStorage

Full Version History

Every sync creates a git commit. Browse your history, view file diffs, and restore any previous version of any file — right inside the app.

Git · Diff viewer

Conflict Resolution

When two devices edit the same file, Obsync detects the conflict and lets you choose: keep local, keep cloud, or keep both — with a live diff preview.

Merge detection · 3-way resolve

Vault Health Check

Detects corrupt git repos, broken remotes, expired tokens, and detached HEADs — then repairs them automatically with one click.

Diagnostics · Auto-repair

Multi-Device Coordination

A lightweight cloud lock file prevents two machines from pushing at the same time. Locks auto-expire after 5 minutes so a crash never leaves your vault stuck.

Sync lock · TTL expiry

How it works

Up and running in 3 steps

No complex setup. No CLI. Just point, connect, and sync.

01

Add your vault

Open Obsync and click "Add Vault". Browse to your Obsidian vault folder — that's it. Obsync initialises a local git repo automatically.

02

Connect a provider

Choose your cloud provider, paste your token or sign in with OAuth, and hit Save. Obsync validates your credentials before saving anything.

03

Push & relax

Hit Push to upload your vault. Enable Auto-Sync and Obsync handles everything from here — watching for changes and syncing in the background.
